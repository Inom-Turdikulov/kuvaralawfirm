What To Do After A California Car Accident

Dealing with the aftermath of a crash can be both stressful and overwhelming. Having to navigate vehicle repairs, medical bills, and lost wages, is no easy feat and it can be hard to know exactly where to start. That is precisely why we created a step-by-step guide to walk you through what to do after a California car accident one step at a time.

Step 3: See A Doctor

Even if you don’t feel pain right away, you should still see a doctor after a car accident. Adrenaline from the accident can mask pain and certain injuries may take a few days to manifest symptoms, so your injuries or condition may actually be worse than you think it is. If you’ve been involved in an accident, it’s worth it to schedule an appointment. Use the map below to find a doctor nearby in your area.

Step 5: Report The Accident To Insurance

If you think your insurance company might not be willing to cover the accident for any reason, you should contact an attorney first before contacting your insurance. However, in any case, you don’t want to delay in contacting insurance. Most insurance companies have policy requirements that detail how quickly you must contact them after an accident. Failure to contact insurance in a timely manner could lead to your claim being rejected. Use the input box below to select your insurance provider and we’ll give you their phone number.

If you don’t have insurance you can still recover compensation. California operates as a No Pay, No Play” state. This means that you won’t be able to recover non-economic damages, but you could still recover compensation for your injuries.

Step 6: Get A Copy Of Your Police Report
As part of gathering documentation on your accident, you’ll want to get a copy of the police report filed if an officer filed a report. You can request a police report using form CHP 190. Click the link below to request a copy of your police report. Request a Police Report
Step 7: Avoid Posting To Social Media
Remember, the insurance company is not your friend. They may monitor you to see if your injuries are really as bad as you say they are. Anything you post on social media can be used against you to deny or reduce your claim. Don’t post anything about the accident on social media. Refrain from creating posts that might hint your injuries aren’t as bad as you say they are.
